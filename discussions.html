Classical Mechanics
Introduction to Lagrangian Mechanics

The Lagrangian mechanics reformulates classical mechanics, providing a powerful method to analyze physical systems. It introduces generalized coordinates and provides a different perspective from Newtonian mechanics.

Hamiltonian Formulation of Classical Mechanics

The Hamiltonian formulation presents another way of expressing classical mechanics. It's especially useful for transitioning to quantum mechanics and is based on energy functions instead of forces.

Quantum Mechanics
The Basics of Quantum Entanglement

Quantum entanglement is a fascinating phenomenon where particles become interconnected such that the state of one particle instantly influences the state of another, regardless of the distance.

Schrodinger's Equation Explained

Schrodinger's equation is the foundation of non-relativistic quantum mechanics. It describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes over time.
